# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

CareerOS is built to help people present their real work honestly and land roles
that fit them. We want the community around it to reflect the same spirit: helpful,
honest, and respectful. Everyone is welcome to take part regardless of background,
identity, experience level, or the kind of job hunt that brought them here.

## What we expect

- Be respectful and assume good faith. Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Be helpful and patient — many people here are new to job-hunting tooling, to
  LaTeX, or to open source. A clear answer beats a dismissive one.
- Be honest. This project refuses to fabricate credentials; we ask the same candour
  of contributors in discussions, issues, and reviews.
- Respect privacy. Never share someone else's personal data, and never paste real
  CVs, contact details, or application materials into public issues or pull requests.

## What is not acceptable

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind, in public or private
  project spaces.
- Personal attacks, demeaning or insulting comments, and sustained disruption of
  discussions.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Encouraging or assisting any of the above.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the repository, issues, pull
requests, discussions, and any channel that represents the project — and when an
individual is representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please report it privately to
**vaibhavkkm@zohomail.in** (more about the maintainer at
<https://www.vaibhavkkm.com>). Reports are handled confidentially. Include what
happened, where, and any context that helps; you will receive an acknowledgement and
an account of how the report was handled.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying these standards and may take any action
they deem appropriate, from a private warning to a temporary or permanent ban from
project spaces, for behaviour they consider inappropriate, threatening, or harmful.
Decisions will be made in good faith and explained to the people involved where it is
reasonable to do so.

These are community guidelines specific to CareerOS; they may evolve as the
project grows.
